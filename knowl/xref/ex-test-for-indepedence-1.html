Example 3.1.9.

Determine whether \(1, \cos^2x,\) and \(\sin^2x\) are linearly independent on \((-\infty, \infty).\)
🔗
Solution.
Suppose there are constants \(c_1, c_2,\) and \(c_3\) that satisfy
\begin{equation*} c_1 + c_2\cos^2x + c_3\sin^2x=0 \end{equation*}
for all \(x\) in \((-\infty, \infty).\) The main question here is: can we have at least one of \(c_1, c_2, c_3\) nonzero? In view of \(\cos^2 x+ \sin^2 x=1\text{,}\) we can take \(c_1 = -1, c_2=c_3 =1.\) Therefore \(1, \cos^2x,\) and \(\sin^2x\) are linearly dependent on \((-\infty, \infty).\)
