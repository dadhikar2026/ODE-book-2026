Exercise 6.
Use Exercise 3.3.4.4 to express a particular solution \(y_p(x)\) of \(y''+k^2 y = f(x),\) where \(k>0\) is in the form
\begin{equation*} y_p(x) = \frac{1}{k}\int_{x_0}^x f(t) \sin(k(x-t))\, dt. \end{equation*}
The integral on the right with \(x_0=0\) is called the convolution of \(f(x)\) and \(\sin (kx),\) in the context of the Laplace transform (see Definition 5.5.1 in Chapter 5).
