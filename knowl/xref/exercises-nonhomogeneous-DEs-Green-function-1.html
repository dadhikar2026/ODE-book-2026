Exercise 4.
Show that \(y_p\) in (3.3.10) may be written as
\begin{equation*} y_p(x) = \int_{x_0}^x G(x, t) f(t) \, dt, \end{equation*}
where \(x, x_0\) are in \(I\) and
\begin{equation*} G(x, t) = \frac{y_1(t) y_2(x) - y_1(x) y_2(t)}{W(t)}=\frac{\begin{vmatrix} y_1(t)&y_2(t)\\ y_1(x)&y_2(x) \end{vmatrix}}{\begin{vmatrix} y_1(t)&y_2(t)\\ y'_1(t)&y'_2(t) \end{vmatrix}} \end{equation*}
with \(W(t) = W(y_1(t), y_2(t))\text{,}\) the Wronskian of \(y_1\) and \(y_2\text{.}\) The function \(G(x, t)\) is called the Green’s function for (3.3.7).
