Theorem 3.1.3. (Existence and Uniqueness).

Let \(a_0(x), \dots, a_{n-1}(x), f(x)\) be continuous functions on some interval \(I\) , \(x_0\) be a point in \(I\text{,}\) and \(y_1,\dots, y_{n-1}\) be fixed numbers. Then the IVP
\begin{equation*} \begin{cases} y^{(n)} + a_{n-1}(x)y^{(n-1)} + \cdots + a_1(x)y' + a_0(x)y = f(x)\\ y(x_0) = y_0,\; y'(x_0) = y_1, \dots, y^{(n-1)}(x_0) = y_{n-1}, \end{cases} \end{equation*}
has a unique solution \(y = \phi(x)\) defined on \(I.\)
