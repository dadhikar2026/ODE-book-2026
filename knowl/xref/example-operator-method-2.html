Example 3.3.15.

Find a particular solution \(y_p\) of
\begin{equation*} y''-5y'+6y= 4xe^{x}. \end{equation*}
🔗
Solution.
In Example 3.3.14, we have
\begin{equation*} y_p = \frac{1}{D-2}\frac{1}{D-3} 4xe^x. \end{equation*}
Suppose \(A_1\) and \(A_2\) are constants for which
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{D-2}\frac{1}{D-3} = \frac{A_1}{D-2}+\frac{A_2}{D-3}. \end{equation*}
Then we find \(A_1= -1\) and \(A_2= 1.\) In view of (3.3.24), we have
\begin{equation*} y_p=-\frac{1}{D-2} 4xe^x+ \frac{1}{D-3}4xe^x, \end{equation*}
which, by (3.3.22) and integration by parts, yields
\begin{equation*} \begin{split} y_p &= -4e^{2x} \int xe^x e^{-2x}\, dx + 4e^{3x} \int xe^x e^{-3x}\, dx\\ &= -4e^{2x} \int x e^{-x}\, dx + 4e^{3x} \int x e^{-2x}\, dx\\ &=e^x(4x+4)+e^x(-2x-1)\\ &=(2x+3)e^x.\end{split} \end{equation*}
