Example 5.1.10.

Compute \(\mathscr{L}\{\mathscr{U}(t-a)\}\) for a nonnegative real number \(a.\)
🔗
Solution.
By Definition 5.1.2, we have
\begin{align*} \mathscr{L} \bigl\{ \mathscr{U}(t-a) \bigr\} \amp = \int_0^{\infty} e^{-st} \mathscr{U}(t-a) \, dt\\ \amp = \int_a^{\infty} e^{-st} \, dt\\ \amp = \left[ \frac{e^{-st}}{-s} \right]_a^\infty \\ = \frac{e^{-as}}{s} \end{align*}
for \(s>0.\) Note that the improper integral defining \(\mathscr{L}\{\mathscr{U}(t-a)\}\) converges only for \(s>0.\) Thus,
\begin{equation*} \boxed{\mathscr{L} \{\mathscr{U}(t-a)\} = \frac{e^{-as}}{s} \quad \text{ for all } s>0.} \end{equation*}
