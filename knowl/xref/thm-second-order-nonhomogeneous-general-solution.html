Theorem 3.3.1. (General Solution).

Consider the nonhomogeneous equation
\begin{equation} y'' + a_1(x)y' + a_0(x)y = f(x),\tag{3.3.1} \end{equation}
where \(a_1\text{,}\) \(a_0\) and \(f\) are continuous functions on an interval \(I\) and the associated homogeneous equation
\begin{equation} y'' + a_1(x)y' + a_0(x)y = 0\tag{3.3.2} \end{equation}
on \(I\text{.}\) Let \(y_h\) be the general solution of (3.3.2) and \(y_p\) be a particular solution of the nonhomogeneous equation (3.3.1) on \(I.\) Then the general solution of (3.3.1) is of the form
\begin{equation} \boxed{y = y_h(x) +y_p(x)}\tag{3.3.3} \end{equation}
on \(I.\)
