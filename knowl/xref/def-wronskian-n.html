Definition 3.1.11. (Wronskian).

Let \(y_1, y_2, \dots, y_n\) be functions that are \(n-1\) times differentiable on an interval \(I.\) Their Wronskian, denoted by \(W(y_1, \dots, y_{n-1})(x)\) (or simply by \(W(x)\)), is a function on \(I\) defined by
\begin{equation*} W(y_1,\dots,y_n)(x) = \begin{vmatrix} y_1(x) & y_2(x) & \cdots & y_n(x)\\ y_1'(x) & y_2'(x) & \cdots & y_n'(x)\\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots\\ y_1^{(n-1)}(x) & y_2^{(n-1)}(x) & \cdots & y_n^{(n-1)}(x) \end{vmatrix} \end{equation*}
on \(I.\)
