Example 5.1.12. Shifting on the \(t-\)axis.

Compute \(\mathscr{L}\{\mathscr{U}(t-a) f(t-a)\}\) for a nonnegative real number \(a.\)
🔗
Solution.
By Definition 5.1.2, we have
\begin{align*} \mathscr{L} \bigl\{ \mathscr{U}(t-a) f(t-a) \bigr\} \amp = \int_0^{\infty} e^{-st} \mathscr{U}(t-a) f(t-a) \, dt\\ \amp = \int_a^{\infty} e^{-st} f(t-a) \, dt\\ \amp = e^{-as}\int_0^\infty e^{-s\tau} f(\tau) d\tau\qquad (\text{letting } \tau = t-a) \\ \amp = e^{-as} \mathscr{L}\{f(t)\}. \end{align*}
Thus, we have
\begin{equation*} \boxed{\mathscr{L} \{\mathscr{U}(t-a) f(t-a)\} = e^{-as} \mathscr{L}\{f(t)\}.} \end{equation*}
