Item 2.
If the known solution \(y_1\) vanishes at some points in the interval \(I\text{,}\) we can first apply the reduction of the order on a subinterval \(J\) of \(I\) where \(y_1\) does not change sign, and then extend the solution to all of \(I\) using a theorem on continuation of solutions (which is beyond the scope of this book). In practice, we may formally compute \(y_2(x)\) using (3.2.19) and then verify that \(y_2\) is actually well-defined on the entire interval \(I\text{.}\) For this, see Example 3.2.11.
